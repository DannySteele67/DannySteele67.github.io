I'm a huge fan of fighting games. One of the more technical aspects of these games that I find fascinating is the way most developers implement a fighting game's netcode now a days. The arguably best, and most used netocode for these games is called Rollback Netcode. It works esentially by predicting a player's inputs and playing them out on the screen. In the cases when the netcode makes a wrong prediction, the game state will be rolled back to the frame where the player made that input and correct the action happening on the screen. This may seem like it would be disorienting to the player, but since these predictions are being made frame by frame the outcome is actually a very smooth online experience.
